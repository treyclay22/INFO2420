5/28/2025

Questions:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Futurosity

www.futurosity.com
  • My first impression was that I was slightly underwhelmed. There are a lot of words on the screen and it feels a little cluttered.
    I think the website looks better/more interesting as you scroll down, though.
  • The information on this website flows downwards. As you scroll, there are links to a book, and to amazon. There are also links at the
    bottom to click on, leading to various social media accounts.
  • I think the general layout of the website makes sense. It is pretty concise and and you scroll, the order of the information just makes sense.
  • While I like the way the information is delivered, I sort of think that some of the graphics don't help. For some reason the picture at
    the very top makes the page look slightly unfinished to me.
    I do like the way the book is displayed though. But like I mentioned earlier there's just generally a large clutter of words.
  • This website loaded very quickly. On mobile as well.
  • I think the text color and size were fine, and I liked the light blue background. I especially like how it switches off with the white background as you scroll
  • There is a scroll bar on the right, and a menu at the top to navigate. Overall it is very effective.
  • There is a definite color palette for this website, that being black, white, and light blue. This was consistent throughout the website and looked great to me.
  • This website responds super well to varying window sizes. On mobile, you still scroll down but the top menu is now tucked away in a sidebar.
    The same is true as you shrink the window on PC. The images and text also sustain their readability very well.
  • I really appreciate the overall structure of the website. While parts of it could look better, there is a very strong flow of
    information while still separating that information into smaller chunks
